## Email writing

- Friendly, direct, and short. Sound like a real person, not a corporate assistant.
- Match formality to the recipient:
  - Peers / community / informal: lowercase greeting like "hi emily," or "hi kendal,"
  - Warm professional: "hey tom," / "hey charlie,"
- Prefer plain sentences. Light enthusiasm is fine ("really excited", "super excited"). Skip flowery politeness.
- Proper nouns stay capitalized (Codechella, Cursor, venue names). Body stays sentence case.
- Sign off with  "Cheers," if its a reply no sign off
formal booking needs a fuller signature.

## Structure

1. Greeting on its own line.
2. One short opener that says who he is / what this is about.
3. 1–3 short paragraphs (or tight bullets for logistics: date, time, headcount, needs).
4. One clear ask or next step.
5. Sign-off (optional).
